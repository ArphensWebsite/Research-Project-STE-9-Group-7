A Comparative Study of Calamansi (Citrus microcarpa) Extract versus Chili (Capsicum annuum) Extract as Organic Pesticides

Chapter 1: Introduction

Background of the Study

In recent years, more people have started using organic farming, which has created a need for safe pest control methods that do not harm the environment or people. Among the natural resources available in the Philippines, Calamansi (Citrus microcarpa) and Chili (Capsicum annuum) have been traditionally used in various applications, including as organic pesticides. But, a comparison of their effectiveness in managing pests, specifically in crops such as snap beans (Phaseolus vulgaris), has yet to be researched

Previous studies have explored the individual pesticidal properties of Calamansi and Chili extracts. For example, research has demonstrated that Calamansi extract possesses insecticidal properties due to its high citric acid content, which acts as a natural deterrent to pests (Muhammad & Iqrar, 2021). Similarly, Chili extract is known for its capsaicin, a compound that has been proven effective in repelling and killing various types of insects.

This research seeks to compare the efficacy of Calamansi and Chili extracts as organic pesticides on snap bean plants. By determining which extract is more effective, this study aims to contribute to the development of eco-friendly pest control strategies that can be applied in agriculture.

Statement of the Problem

This study seeks to evaluate the effectiveness of Calamansi extract and Chili extract as organic pesticides in controlling pests that affect snap bean plants.

Specifically, it seeks to answer the following questions:

  • What is the mortality rate of pests when snap bean plants are treated with Calamansi extract?
  • What is the mortality rate of pests when snap bean plants are treated with Chili extract?
  • Does the concentration of the extract affect tne mortality rate of pests on snap beans?

Hypotheses

  1. There are no significant differences between the pesticidal properties of calamansi and chili extracts.
  2. There is a significant differences between the pesticidal properties of calamansi and chili extracts.

Definition of Terms

  • CalamansiCitrus microcarpa Extract: The liquid obtained from pressing the fruit of the Citrus microcarpa plant, known for its pesticidal properties due to its high content of essential oils.
  • ChiliCapsicum annuum Extract: The liquid obtained from pressing the fruit of the Capsicum annuum plant, containing capsaicin, which acts as a natural deterrent to pests.

This study is important because it helps us learn how natural things like Calamansi and chili can be used to protect plants from bugs without using harmful chemicals. By understanding how these natural extracts work as pesticides, farmers can keep their crops healthy in a way that's safe for the environment and for people. This could lead to healthier food and a cleaner planet!
